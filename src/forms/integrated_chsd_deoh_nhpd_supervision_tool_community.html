C. Refer to health facility
Explain why the child needs to go to the health facility. Give first dose of treatment.  Do not touch child.
IF DIARRHEA (14 days or more OR blood in stool)
If child can drink, give ORS right away
IF FEVER (7 days or more)
Give one dose of paracetamol for fever.
Give one dose of oral antimalarial (ACT) (see dose card).
Teach caregiver to give first dose of ACT now. Advise to give next doses for 2 more days if unable to refer to facility.
Advise a sponge bath and keep child as cool as possible.
IF CHEST INDRAWING
If child can drink, give first dose of oral antibiotics (amoxicillin) (see dose card)
For any sick child who can drink, advise to give fluids and keep feeding.
Advise to keep child warm, if no fever.
Complete "FHW Form: Referral". Arrange transport and referral.

D. Treat at home and advise caregiver
IF DIARRHEA (less than 14 days and no blood in stool)
Give ORS. Help caregiver to give child ORS in front of you until child is not thirsty.
Give caregiver 2 ORS packets to use at home. Advise to give as much as the child wants, but at least 1/2 cup ORS solution after each stool (poo poo).
Give 1 dose of zinc supplement daily for 10 days (see dose card). Help caregiver give first dose now.
IF FEVER (less than 7 days)
Do not take temp. Do not do RDT.
Give one dose of paracetamol for fever.
Give one dose antimalarial (ACT) for fever (see dose card).
Teach caregiver to give first dose now. Advise to give next dose for 2 more days.
Advise sponge bath and keep child as cool as possible.
IF FAST BREATHING (pneumonia)
Give oral antibiotic (amoxicillin) two times a day for five days (see dose card).
Teach caregiver to give first dose now.
Advise the caregiver to give more fluids and continue feeding.
E. If OTHER PROBLEM you cannot treat, refer child to facility. Complete "FHW Form: Referral"
