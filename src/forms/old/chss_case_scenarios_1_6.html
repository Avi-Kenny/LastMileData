Case Scenarios: Round 3, CHSS v.1.6
Correct Treatment Rate Assessment
These scenarios should be used by quality assurance personnel in the field. Case scenarios should be read word for word to the CHSS , and no information should be given to the CHSS unless they ask for it (or unless otherwise stated). You must follow the instructions in the guidelines for evaluators. Forms should be submitted to RM&E within 2 weeks of the assessment taking place.

Place the job aid, MUAC, RDT kit, DCT or other timer, paper sick child and referral forms in the evaluation area. Then, r ead the following out loud to the CHSS :

“The reason for this activity is for us to see how well you are knowing about how a CHA should be doing their everyday work. I know you are a clinical person who has your own way of evaluating and treating the sick child. But for this exercise, you should follow the protocols that the CHAs are using.

In each of the cases, I will pretend to be a caregiver coming to the CHA with a sick child. You should pretend you are the CHA. You can use the job aid, paper forms, DCT, and all the tools a CHA has to help you do your work.

The same normal way you can coach the CHA to do their work, that's how you should feel free to be in front of us. Please fill out the paper sick child form for every case. For the name of the child, you can write the case number (“Case 1.”)

Ask any questions you want to ask about the sick child. If you want to check the child, tell me what exactly you want to LOOK for and I will SHOW you or TELL you what you see. We want you to know that you are in control and can decide whether to refer or not. All the drugs you need are in your box.

Take your own time on each of the case, because when you are finished with one you will not go back to it. Do you have any questions?”

Before beginning the case scenarios, answer any questions the CHSS has . If the CHSS makes mistakes , please note the mistakes and go over them after ALL case scenarios have been performed. Do not prompt or lead the CHSS to do the right thing.
