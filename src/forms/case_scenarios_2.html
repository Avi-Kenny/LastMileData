Read the following out loud to the CHA:

“The reason for these cases, we will be reading to you is for us to see how you can do your everyday work.

In each of the cases, I will pretend to be a caregiver coming to you, the CHA, with a sick child. From there na, you start asking all your questions. You can use the job aid, paper forms, phone, and all the tools you have to help you do your work.

The same normal way you use your tools and do your everyday work, that's how you should feel free during this activity. If you can always use the DCT first while asking the caregiver, do it. If that the paper forms first, do it.

Ask any questions you want to ask about the sick child like you would if this really was a sick child and their caregiver coming to you for advice or treatment. Do not assume or guess any information – For example, if you need to know if the child can be hot, you must ask me and only then will I tell you. If you want to check the child by doing physical exam or tests, tell me what exactly you want to LOOK for and I will SHOW you or TELL you what you see. You can decide whether to refer or not. If you want to refer the child, make the referral in your normal way.

Take your own time on each of the case, because when you are finished with one you will not go back to it. Do you have any questions?”
